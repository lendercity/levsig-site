The Basics

MSTU provides 2x daily leveraged exposure to MicroStrategy (MSTR) — a business intelligence company that has transformed itself into essentially a Bitcoin holding company. CONL provides 2x daily leveraged exposure to Coinbase (COIN) — the largest publicly traded US cryptocurrency exchange.

How Each Gets Its Crypto Exposure

MSTU — Bitcoin by proxy

MicroStrategy holds over 200,000 Bitcoin on its balance sheet, making its stock price essentially a leveraged Bitcoin proxy. When Bitcoin rises 5%, MSTR often rises 10-15% due to its concentrated Bitcoin exposure and premium valuation. MSTU then adds another 2x on top of that — making it effectively a 4-6x Bitcoin instrument in strong trends.

This stacking of leverage creates extraordinary upside in Bitcoin bull cycles — and equally extraordinary downside in bear markets. MSTU can lose 40-60% of its value during Bitcoin corrections without Bitcoin itself falling that dramatically.

CONL — Crypto exchange revenue

Coinbase's business model is driven by crypto trading volume and transaction fees. When crypto markets are active — prices rising, retail investors engaged, volatility high — Coinbase generates significant revenue. When crypto enters a bear market or low-volatility period, trading volume drops and Coinbase's earnings fall sharply.

CONL is less directly tied to Bitcoin price than MSTU, but highly correlated to overall crypto market sentiment. It also has exposure to Coinbase's regulatory environment, product expansion, and institutional crypto adoption trends that MSTU doesn't have.

Both carry HIGH decay risk. The underlying volatility of MSTR and COIN is among the highest of any stocks in the leveraged ETF universe. Daily moves of 5-15% are not uncommon. In choppy crypto markets, MSTU and CONL can decay rapidly even when Bitcoin eventually goes sideways. Position size small and exit quickly when trend signals break.

Volatility Comparison

MSTU is generally more volatile than CONL because MSTR's price is amplified by its Bitcoin premium — it moves more than Bitcoin itself. During major Bitcoin rallies or crashes, MSTU can move 20-30% in a single week at the 2x leverage level.

CONL is volatile but slightly more anchored to fundamental business performance. Coinbase's earnings reports, regulatory news (SEC actions, exchange licenses), and product launches create their own catalysts independent of pure Bitcoin price movement.

Signal Behavior

Both ETFs respond to systematic trend signals but require more patience due to their volatile underlying nature. Crypto markets run 24/7 while signals are generated on EOD equity data — overnight Bitcoin moves can affect next-day signal readings significantly.

MSTU signals tend to cluster around Bitcoin trend cycles. Extended Bitcoin bull markets produce sustained MSTU uptrends. Bitcoin sideways action produces rapid decay and frequent stop-outs.

CONL signals are influenced by both crypto market conditions and Coinbase-specific events. Earnings seasons can produce large moves that may conflict with or confirm the technical trend signal.

Our take: Both are in the LevSig signal universe but rated LOWER underlying quality — the signals are valid, but position sizing should reflect the higher risk. These are complementary to core positions in TQQQ, SOXL, and TECL — not replacements for them. Never allocate more than a small percentage of your leveraged ETF exposure to either.

Bottom Line

MSTU and CONL are not for the faint-hearted. Both offer extraordinary upside in crypto bull cycles and equally extraordinary downside when conditions reverse. The discipline of systematic entry and exit signals matters more here than with any other ETF class — holding either through a crypto winter without a defined exit rule is one of the fastest ways to lose significant capital.

Trade them with small position sizes, wide ATR stops that respect their volatility, and strict adherence to exit signals. When the trend breaks, get out. The next signal will come.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is MSTU ETF?

MSTU is the T-Rex 2X Long MSTR Daily Target ETF. It provides 2x daily leveraged exposure to MicroStrategy (MSTR), a company that holds Bitcoin as its primary treasury asset. MSTU moves approximately 2x the daily return of MSTR, which itself is highly correlated to Bitcoin price movements.

What is CONL ETF?

CONL is the GraniteShares 2X Long Coinbase Daily ETF. It provides 2x daily leveraged exposure to Coinbase (COIN), the largest publicly traded US cryptocurrency exchange. CONL moves approximately 2x the daily return of COIN, which is correlated to overall crypto market activity and trading volume.

Is MSTU or CONL riskier?

Both carry extremely high risk. MSTU is arguably riskier because MicroStrategy's stock already behaves like leveraged Bitcoin before MSTU adds another 2x on top — making it effectively a 4-6x Bitcoin instrument. CONL's risk is driven by crypto trading volume and the regulatory environment around exchanges.

How much should I allocate to MSTU or CONL?

Due to their extreme volatility and decay risk, both should be a small percentage of any leveraged ETF portfolio — typically no more than 5-10% of your total leveraged ETF exposure. The high volatility means your ATR stop covers a larger dollar range, so smaller position size is essential to keep dollar risk per trade manageable.
